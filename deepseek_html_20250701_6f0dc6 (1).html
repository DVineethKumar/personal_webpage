Projects

Design and Fabrication of Indigenous Piezoelectric Accelerometer Sensor

Duration: 12 months | Supervisor: Dr. Govind N Sahu

Currently working on the design of a piezoelectric accelerometer for vibration measurement in dynamic systems. Focused on material selection, sensitivity optimization, and frequency response analysis through simulation models. Intended for application in machine dynamics with an emphasis on low-cost, high-sensitivity sensor design.

Tools Used: COMSOL Multiphysics, MATLAB, Ansys Workbench, Arduino IDE

Design and Optimization of Disc Brake Rotor

Duration: 6 months

Developed novel vented and slotted rotor profiles and compared them against conventional designs to assess performance under braking conditions. Conducted structural and thermal simulations using finite element analysis to evaluate stress, temperature distribution, and deformation. Results showed that the new profiles offered improved heat dissipation and reduced thermal distortion, leading to enhanced braking efficiency and durability.

Tools Used: PTC Creo, ANSYS

Design and Optimization of Mechanical Rover

Duration: 2 months

Designed a multi-terrain mechanical rover and performed structural and stability optimization for enhanced durability and performance. Modeled 3D geometry in Creo and carried out static and dynamic analysis in ANSYS.

Tools Used: PTC Creo, ANSYS

Design and Fabrication of Solar Powered Hacksaw

Duration: 6 months

Developed a solar-powered hacksaw using a DC motor and crank-slider mechanism for light-duty metal cutting. Modeled components in SolidWorks and integrated solar panels with battery storage for sustainable operation.

Tools Used: SolidWorks
